# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

People standing at a life threshold — corporate professionals who feel trapped by their own competence, empaths and caretakers running on empty, those who feel like outsiders even in their own families or relationships. They are intellectually sophisticated (often analytical, high-performing) and skeptical of generic wellness. They arrive knowing something needs to shift, but not quite sure what or how. Context: they're probably reading late at night, alone, after a day of performing composure.

## Product Purpose

A single-page practice site for Nathan Ghabour, somatic healer and former AI/ML tech executive (Nike, Amazon). The site exists to establish trust, communicate the specific texture of Nathan's work (Reiki + vibrational sound therapy, Remote Medicine Story & Guidance, Sacred Hapé Ceremony), and convert a curious visitor into a free 15-minute consultation booking. Success means the visitor feels recognized — not sold to.

## Brand Personality

Grounded, ceremonial, liminal. The tone is warm without being soft, intellectually honest without being clinical. Nathan's path FROM the analytical world to somatic practice is the story — the brand holds both without apologizing for either. Voice: unhurried, specific, non-jargony spiritual. Never performs healing; it simply describes it.

## Anti-references

- **Tech-bro minimalism** — Cold white, Helvetica, $5k/hr executive coaching aesthetics. This is the world Nathan came from; the design must consciously move away from it.
- **Wellness-marketplace clichés** — Soft lavender, lotus imagery, Om symbols, stock "healing energy" photography. Generic spiritual-practitioner look.
- **Clinical therapeutic** — BetterHelp / Headspace territory. Safety-optimized pastels, trust-by-committee visual language.

## Design Principles

1. **The body leads.** Design should feel felt before it's read. Warmth, organic curves, unhurried pacing — the visual rhythm mirrors the somatic practice itself.
2. **Not the healer you expect.** Nathan's analytical background is a feature, not a contradiction. Refuse both the corporate-premium and the spiritual-cliché lane simultaneously.
3. **Liminal means in-between.** Transitions between sections are design moments, not seams. Edges, thresholds, and passages deserve intentional craft.
4. **Earned neon.** The vibrant accents (volt, magenta, teal, ember) are not decorative. They mark contact points between the ordinary and the extraordinary — use them with restraint and purpose.
5. **One primary truth at a time.** One primary CTA per viewport. One headline per section. The visitor should never wonder what they're supposed to feel or do next.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Keyboard navigable. Screen reader support for all interactive elements. `prefers-reduced-motion` respected for animations (FoilArc shimmer, accordion transitions).
